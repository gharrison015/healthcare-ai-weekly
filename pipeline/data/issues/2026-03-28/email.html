Healthcare AI Weekly

Week of March 21 - March 28, 2026
Healthcare AI is hitting an execution wall. While everyone talks about Shadow AI and nurses get their first real EHR-integrated copilots, the industry's bigger challenge is becoming clear: we're great at pilots, terrible at scale. Meanwhile, Anthropic's $400M biotech bet and prior auth reform's measly 11% progress show the gap between AI hype and healthcare reality.

This Week's Impact
Shadow AI Spreads Inside Health Systems
Wolters Kluwer survey shows unauthorized AI use is widespread in healthcare organizations, with staff using AI tools for clinical work without IT approval. This creates compliance, security, and liability risks that leadership often can't see. Health systems need governance frameworks to manage shadow AI rather than trying to stop it entirely.

Ambience Cracks Nursing AI Integration
Ambience Healthcare launched Chart Chat, the first EHR-integrated AI copilot built specifically for nurses. Cleveland Clinic nurses in the pilot say it helps them build better patient understanding. Unlike physician AI tools adapted for nursing, this addresses nursing-specific workflows within existing EHR systems.

Emerging
AI Scribes Drive Up Healthcare Costs
Insurers and hospitals privately agree that AI scribes are driving up healthcare costs by enabling more detailed documentation and higher-level billing codes. While physicians love the tools for reducing administrative burden, the cost increases are forcing difficult conversations about ROI and potential payer responses.

Prior Auth Reform Falls Flat
Major insurers committed to prior authorization reform last summer but have only eliminated 11% of prior auths so far. The minimal progress suggests voluntary reform won't meaningfully reduce administrative burden, potentially accelerating demand for AI automation tools and regulatory intervention.

Counsel Health Expands AI Primary Care
Counsel Health is expanding its AI-powered primary care model from basic services to lifestyle and chronic condition management throughout 2026. This tests whether AI-first care delivery can effectively manage complex patients while reducing costs for value-based contracts.

Waystar Builds AI Revenue Recovery Tool
Waystar developed an AI solution to identify and help recover provider revenue lost to payer 'take-backs' or payment recoupments. The tool uses pattern recognition to spot questionable clawbacks and automate contesting them, addressing a common but hard-to-track revenue leak.

Oracle Health AI Cuts Documentation Time
Southwest General used Oracle Health's Clinical AI Agent to generate 81,800 clinical notes while reducing physicians' after-hours documentation work. The implementation demonstrates production-scale AI deployment with measurable improvements in physician work-life balance and administrative efficiency.

Epic Integrates Galleri Cancer Screening
GRAIL's Galleri multi-cancer blood test is now integrated directly into Epic's EHR platform, giving 450+ health systems seamless ordering capability. The integration eliminates workflow barriers that typically slow new test adoption and could significantly impact population health screening programs.
