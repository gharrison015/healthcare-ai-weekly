This Week's Impact
Health Systems Dump EHR AI Roadmaps
Qventus found only 22% of health system CIOs will wait for EHR vendor AI features, down from 52% in 2025. The survey of 60+ senior IT leaders shows health systems are prioritizing AI deployment speed over EHR integration, with 94% saying delays create competitive disadvantage.

Cleveland Clinic Bets Big on Luminai Platform
San Francisco-based Luminai raised $38M Series B and announced a massive enterprise AI deployment with Cleveland Clinic across 15 million patients and 23 hospitals. The partnership focuses on horizontal AI automation rather than point solutions, with Peak XV Partners leading the funding round.

Epic Launches County Disease Surveillance
Epic debuted a new feature that monitors county-level health trends across its network and issues alerts to providers when elevated illness rates are detected. The system turns EHR data into real-time public health intelligence for capacity planning and outbreak detection.

Emerging
Trump Admin Targets Drug Prior Auth Timelines
The Trump administration proposed a CMS rule extending prior authorization interoperability requirements to pharmaceuticals with shortened decision deadlines and expanded public reporting. The rule builds on 2024's framework that covered medical services, now targeting drug approvals.

80% of Clinicians Trust Evidence-Based AI Tools
EBSCO Clinical Decisions found 80% of clinicians trust evidence-based AI clinical decision support tools, with 89% believing they improve patient outcomes. However, 64% of patients prefer human clinicians present when AI influences care decisions, creating a trust gap that affects deployment strategies.
